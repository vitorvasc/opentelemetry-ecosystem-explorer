## Research Plan

### Research Goals

1. Understand how users currently find information about OpenTelemetry components and
   instrumentation.
2. Identify the key questions users want answered when evaluating a component.
3. Understand differences in information needs across user personas (developers, platform engineers,
   SREs).
4. Discover pain points and gaps in the current information-finding experience.
5. Understand how users incorporate LLMs into their information-seeking workflow.

### Research Questions

#### Goal 1: How users currently find information

- When users need information about an OpenTelemetry component, where do they look first?
- What sources do users trust most when researching components?
- What challenges do users encounter when searching for component information?
- What information is hardest to find?

#### Goal 2: Key questions users want answered

- What information do users need before deciding to use a component?
- How do users determine whether a component meets their needs?
- What questions do users commonly ask about instrumentation libraries, collectors, or integrations?
- What information helps users feel confident implementing a component?

#### Goal 3: Understand differences in needs

- How do the information needs of application developers differ from those of platform engineers and
  SREs?
- What tasks are users trying to accomplish when researching components?
- What information is most valuable to each persona?
- How do users' workflows influence the way they consume documentation and technical information?

#### Goal 4: Improve the Ecosystem Explorer experience

- What frustrations do users experience when evaluating OpenTelemetry components?
- What features would make component discovery and evaluation easier?
- What would an ideal component information page include?
- How could the Ecosystem Explorer better support users' workflows and decision-making?

#### Goal 5: Use of LLMs

- How do users currently use LLMs when working with OpenTelemetry components?
- At what stages of their workflow do users rely on LLMs?
- What advantages do users perceive when using LLMs compared to traditional documentation or search
  methods?
- What frustrations, limitations, or inaccuracies have users experienced with LLM-generated
  responses?

#### Research Method

The primary research method will be user interviews. User interviews allow participants to describe
their experiences in their own words while ensuring that key research topics are consistently
covered across sessions. Participants will be selected to represent different user types, including:

- Application Developers
- Platform Engineers
- Site Reliability Engineers (SREs)
- OpenTelemetry Contributors / Maintainers (For stakeholder insights) This purposive sampling
  approach ensures that a range of perspectives and information needs are represented.
