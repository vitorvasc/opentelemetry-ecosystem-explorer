## Approach for the UX Research & IA for Ecosystem Explorer Project

My approach to this project follows a structured, research-first process. Beginning with domain
familiarisation, moving through exploratory research, competitive analysis, and stakeholder and user
interviews, which will result in clear information architecture recommendations for the Ecosystem
Explorer. Rather than jumping straight to solutions, I will first build an understanding of the
OpenTelemetry ecosystem, existing user workflows, and how similar platforms organise dense technical
information, ensuring every recommendation is grounded in real user needs and validated patterns.

1. **Project Framing & Domain Familiarisation** I will begin by building a clear understanding of
   the OpenTelemetry ecosystem and the Ecosystem Explorer. This includes reviewing core concepts
   such as instrumentation, collectors, exporters, and telemetry signals; exploring how different
   ecosystems structure instrumentation information; and reviewing GitHub issues and community
   discussions to understand known pain points and design intent. Outcome: A clear mental model of
   the domain and problem space.

2. **Initial Information Mapping**. Before formal research, I will conduct a lightweight review of
   existing documentation and systems to understand how users currently discover instrumentation and
   components, where information is fragmented, and how similar platforms structure technical
   content. This will shape early hypotheses and guide interview questions. Outcome: Initial
   understanding of workflows and information gaps.

3. **Competitive & Comparative Analysis** I will analyse similar platforms such as npm, Docker Hub,
   crates.io, and Go package documentation, focusing on information hierarchy, navigation and
   discovery patterns, filtering and search, and versioning presentation. Outcome: Relevant best
   practices and design patterns for the Explorer.

4. **Stakeholder Interviews** I will engage with project maintainers and contributors to understand
   the goals and vision of the Ecosystem Explorer, known constraints, and success criteria. Findings
   will also inform my research screener and interview guide. Outcome: Alignment between project
   intent and user needs.

5. **Research Preparation** Before user interviews, I will define research goals, identify
   participant groups, and develop discussion guides and consent documentation. Materials will be
   refined based on mentor feedback before outreach begins. Outcome: A structured research plan.

6. **User Research** I will conduct interviews with application developers, platform engineers, and
   SREs to understand how they discover and evaluate instrumentation, what information they need at
   different decision points, and where current workflows break down. A survey may be introduced to
   validate qualitative patterns across a wider group if needed. Outcome: Information about
   real-world workflows, needs, and pain points.

7. **Analysis & Synthesis** I will synthesise findings by identifying patterns across interviews,
   mapping workflows and decision-making behaviours, developing user personas, and comparing
   findings against stakeholder assumptions. Outcome: Clear research insights and user models.

8. **Information Architecture Recommendations** Based on research findings, I will propose a
   structured taxonomy, information hierarchy for component pages, and navigation and discovery
   improvements. Recommendations will be reviewed with mentors and refined before finalisation.
   Outcome: A user-centred structure for scaling the Ecosystem Explorer.

9. **Design Exploration (Stretch Goal)** I will translate IA recommendations into wireframes
   illustrating improved discovery flows, ecosystem navigation, and progressive disclosure of
   technical information. Outcome: Visual validation of the proposed structure.

10. **Iteration & Communication** Throughout the project I will share progress with mentors at each
    phase of the process and incorporate feedback into outputs. Findings will also be documented
    through blog posts at the early stage, mid-research, and post-synthesis phases.
